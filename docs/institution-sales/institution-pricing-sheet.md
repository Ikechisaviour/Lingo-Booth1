# Draft Institution Pricing Sheet

These prices are draft ranges for conversation with a first university customer. The goal is to give the buyer a concrete structure while keeping room for founding-institution terms.

## Pilot Pricing

| Option | Best For | Included Seats | Term | Draft Price |
| --- | --- | ---: | --- | ---: |
| Founding Pilot | First university cohort | Up to 100 | 6 weeks | $2,500 |
| Expanded Pilot | Multiple sections or offices | Up to 250 | 8 weeks | $5,000 |
| Custom Pilot | Larger or multi-language launch | Custom | 6-12 weeks | Quote |

**Pilot credit:** Apply the paid pilot fee toward the first annual subscription if the university signs within 30 days of the pilot review.

## Annual Institution Plans

| Plan | Best For | Seat Range | Draft Annual Price | Effective Seat Price |
| --- | --- | ---: | ---: | ---: |
| Institution Plus | One class, club, or small program | 50-100 seats | $6,000-$10,000 | ~$100-$120/year |
| Institution Pro | Department or study abroad office | 101-500 seats | $12,000-$35,000 | ~$70-$120/year |
| Institution Premium | Multi-department or campus program | 501+ seats | Custom | Volume-based |

## Optional Commercial Terms

- Founding institution discount: 20-30% off first year in exchange for structured feedback and permission to use anonymized results.
- Multi-year discount: 10-15% off annual pricing for a 2-year term.
- Semester license: 60% of annual price for a single academic term.
- Department expansion clause: apply pilot or first-cohort fees toward a larger annual agreement.
- Support package: include onboarding and end-of-term reporting in Pro/Premium plans.

## Pricing Conversation Guidance

Open with the pilot, not the annual license:

"For a first university partner, I would recommend a paid 6-week pilot for up to 100 learners. That lets us prove engagement with a real cohort, support the instructors closely, and credit the pilot fee toward an annual plan if it works."

If they ask for per-seat pricing:

"For institutions, we usually price by annual seat bundles rather than individual consumer subscriptions. The effective rate depends on cohort size, support needs, and whether this is one department or a broader campus program."

If they say budget is limited:

"We can scope the first pilot tightly around one cohort and one success metric. The most important thing is to make the first deployment measurable and easy for faculty to support."

## Information To Confirm Before Sending Final Pricing

- Number of learners in the first cohort
- Number of instructors or administrators
- Target language programs
- Whether they need web only, mobile only, or both
- Reporting expectations
- Procurement path and contract requirements
- Whether this is department-funded, grant-funded, or campus-funded
- Desired launch date
