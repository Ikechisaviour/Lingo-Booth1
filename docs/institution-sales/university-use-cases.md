# University-Specific Use Cases For Lingo Booth

## Use Case 1: Language Department Practice Companion

**Audience:** Students enrolled in Korean, Spanish, Chinese, Japanese, French, German, or other language courses.

**Problem:** Students need more practice between class meetings, but instructors have limited time to create individualized drills, speaking prompts, and review paths.

**Lingo Booth fit:** Students use guided lessons, conversation practice, flashcards, quizzes, and writing practice outside class. Instructors can position the platform as structured weekly practice that supports classroom instruction without replacing the teacher.

**Pilot shape:** One course section or level group for 6 weeks, with weekly recommended practice goals and a final engagement summary.

**What the university learns:** Whether students practice more consistently, which modes they actually use, and whether the platform reduces the gap between motivated learners and learners who need more structure.

## Use Case 2: Study Abroad Readiness Program

**Audience:** Students preparing for semester abroad, exchange programs, faculty-led trips, internships, or international placements.

**Problem:** Learners often need practical language confidence before travel, especially for greetings, directions, daily routines, university life, housing, food, transport, and workplace interactions.

**Lingo Booth fit:** Lingo Booth can support destination-specific preparation through guided lessons and conversation scenarios, while still working from the learner's own native language.

**Pilot shape:** A 4-6 week pre-departure cohort with a practical target such as "complete survival communication practice before arrival."

**What the university learns:** Whether students arrive with stronger confidence and whether the study abroad office can provide scalable preparation without running a full language course.

## Use Case 3: International Student And Campus Support

**Audience:** Incoming international students, multilingual student support programs, or campus offices serving learners who need English or local-language support.

**Problem:** Students may need flexible practice for academic, social, and administrative situations, but campus staff cannot provide one-to-one practice for every language background.

**Lingo Booth fit:** Because Lingo Booth supports multiple native and target languages, learners can practice from their own language into the language they need for campus life.

**Pilot shape:** A support-office cohort focused on campus communication: introductions, appointments, asking for help, class participation, housing, health services, and workplace-style interactions.

**What the university learns:** Whether the platform improves self-directed preparation and gives support teams another resource for students who need practice outside office hours.

## Use Case 4: Continuing Education Or Community Language Program

**Audience:** Adult learners, alumni, community education students, or certificate-program participants.

**Problem:** Continuing education programs need affordable, engaging digital practice that can accompany paid courses or stand alone between live sessions.

**Lingo Booth fit:** Lingo Booth gives adult learners structured practice paths, mobile access, and progress tracking that can be paired with live instruction.

**Pilot shape:** A paid small cohort with optional instructor office hours and a renewal path into a semester or annual license.

**What the university learns:** Whether Lingo Booth can become an add-on to revenue-generating non-degree language programs.

## Best First Use Case Recommendation

For the first paying institution client, lead with **Use Case 1: Language Department Practice Companion** unless the university has already named a study abroad or international office need. It is the easiest to measure, easiest to explain to faculty, and easiest to convert into an annual department subscription.
