# Lingo Booth University Pilot Proposal

## Recommended Pilot Shape

**Pilot length:** 6 weeks  
**Pilot cohort:** 50-100 active learners  
**Primary audience:** One language department, study abroad cohort, international office program, or continuing education group  
**Access:** Web and mobile access for learners, instructor/admin access for university stakeholders  
**Languages:** Any supported native-to-target pair, with the first pilot scoped to the university's highest-priority language program  
**Commercial model:** Paid pilot, credited toward an annual institution subscription if the university continues

## Pilot Objective

Help the university measure whether Lingo Booth increases consistent out-of-class language practice, gives learners more ways to practice speaking and writing, and gives program stakeholders clearer visibility into learner engagement and progress.

## Included In The Pilot

- Learner accounts for the agreed pilot cohort
- Guided lessons and class-style practice
- Conversation practice with a guided practice partner
- Flashcards, quizzes, writing practice, and progress tracking
- Web and mobile access
- Institution dashboard access where available
- Setup support for the pilot coordinator
- Midpoint check-in and end-of-pilot usage summary

## Suggested Success Metrics

- Activation: at least 75% of invited learners create accounts
- Engagement: at least 60% of activated learners complete two or more practice sessions per week
- Practice depth: learners use at least three learning modes during the pilot
- Instructor value: program staff can identify participation patterns and learner needs from available reporting
- Continuation signal: the university identifies a next cohort, department, or term for expansion

## Pilot Timeline

1. **Week 0 - Setup**
   Confirm cohort, language pair, access method, pilot coordinator, and reporting needs.

2. **Week 1 - Launch**
   Onboard learners, share access instructions, and confirm that instructors can review the agreed progress signals.

3. **Weeks 2-3 - Usage Build**
   Monitor activation, answer support questions, and tune the recommended study flow for the cohort.

4. **Week 4 - Midpoint Review**
   Review early engagement, adoption blockers, and any instructor requests before the final stretch.

5. **Weeks 5-6 - Completion**
   Encourage final usage, collect learner/instructor feedback, and prepare a concise summary.

6. **Week 7 - Expansion Decision**
   Discuss whether to renew, expand to another cohort, or move into a department subscription.

## University Responsibilities

- Name one pilot coordinator
- Confirm the pilot cohort and target language program
- Share learner invitation list or preferred onboarding method
- Define what success should look like for the department
- Attend launch, midpoint, and final review meetings

## Lingo Booth Responsibilities

- Configure the pilot access
- Support onboarding and usage questions
- Provide the learning platform across web and mobile
- Share usage and progress summary at the end of the pilot
- Recommend the best-fit subscription option after the pilot

## Proposed Pilot Fee

**Recommended founding-institution pilot:** $2,500 for up to 100 learners for 6 weeks.  
If the university converts to an annual subscription within 30 days of the final review, the pilot fee is credited toward the first-year subscription.
