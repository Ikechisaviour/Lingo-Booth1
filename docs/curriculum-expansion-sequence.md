# Curriculum Expansion Sequence

This sequence is the working order for taking every target-language curriculum from beginner-complete to genuinely multi-level complete.

## Why This Order

The order favors:

1. Languages closest to an existing mature family model.
2. Languages whose Level 1 authoring is already comparatively strong.
3. Language families where one authoring pass can teach the next pass without flattening language-specific differences.

## Sequence

1. `it`, `pt`
   - Extend the Romance-family work without copying Spanish distinctions blindly.
   - Build target-specific advanced clusters for article use, pronouns, tense/aspect, mood, and register.
2. `nl`
   - Reuse lessons learned from Germanic authoring while preserving Dutch word order, separable verbs, diminutives, and modal particles.
3. `ms`, `id`
   - Build Austronesian depth around affixation, reduplication, classifiers, politeness, and formal/informal register.
4. `fil`
   - Treat focus/voice marking, linker use, particles, and register as first-class features rather than a Malay/Indonesian clone.
5. `tr`
   - Build around agglutination, vowel harmony, evidentiality, participles, and politeness.
6. `ru`
   - Build around case, aspect, motion verbs, stress, and register.
7. `ar`, `he`
   - Build Semitic depth with root-pattern morphology, construct-state or iḍāfa-like structures, register, and script-specific pronunciation.
8. `hi`, `bn`, `ta`
   - Build South Asian depth with script, agreement, case/postpositions, honorifics, and spoken-versus-formal contrasts where they matter.

## Completion Criteria Per Language

A language is not complete until it has:

1. The full 51-lesson shape:
   - Level 1 foundation + Units 1-21
   - Level 2 thematic units + reviews
   - Level 2 adult/workplace units
   - Level 3 target-specific grammar clusters
2. Target-language-specific grammar and pronunciation features, not generic copied labels.
3. Rich canonical glosses and no target/native-layer contamination.
4. Learner-flow spot checks on at least:
   - `en -> target`
   - one non-English native language into the target
   - one script-different pair when relevant
5. Passing curriculum, localization, and parity audits before any database seed.

## Non-Goal

The goal is not to make every curriculum identical. The goal is to make every curriculum equally serious.
